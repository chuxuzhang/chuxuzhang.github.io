About
I have been an Associate Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Connecticut since 2024. My research interests center around machine learning and data science. Recently, I have focused on developing foundational, resource-efficient, and safe machine learning algorithms and models, particularly on graph and text data. Besides, I apply them to applications in various domains including public health/healthcare, social media, recommender systems, and natural language processing. My works are majorly published in top conferences of machine learning and data science (e.g., ICML, NeurIPS, ICLR, and KDD). I direct the Machine Intelligence and Data Science (MINDS) Lab.

I have received some awards and honors such as IJCAI Early Career Spotlight (2025), ICBS Frontiers of Science Award (2024), and NSF CAREER Award (2024). Besides, my work won several Best Paper Awards or nominations in leading conferences.

Before joining UConn, I was an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Brandeis University (2020-2024). I completed my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Notre Dame (2017-2020), advised by Professor Nitesh Chawla.

My family lives in the Greater New York area.
