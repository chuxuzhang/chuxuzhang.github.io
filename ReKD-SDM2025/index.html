Introduction
Modern knowledge discovery and machine learning techniques, especially deep neural networks and large language models, have demonstrated excellent performance for various applications. However, the development of many of these techniques still encounters resource constraint challenges in many scenarios, such as limited labeled data (data-level), small model size requirements in real-world computing platforms (model-level), and efficient mapping of the computations to heterogeneous target hardware (system-level). Addressing all of these metrics is critical for the effective and efficient usage of the developed models in a wide variety of real systems, such as large-scale social network analysis, large-scale recommendation systems, and real-time anomaly detection. Therefore, it is desirable to develop resource-efficient knowledge discovery techniques to tackle challenges of resource limitations from data, model, or (and) system perspectives. The proposed international workshop on "Resource-efficient Knowledge Discovery" (ReKD) will provide a great venue at SDM 2025 for academic researchers and industrial practitioners to share challenges, solutions, and future opportunities of resource-efficient knowledge discovery.
The goal of this workshop is to create a venue to tackle the challenges that arise when modern knowledge discovery and machine learning techniques (e.g., deep neural networks and large language models) encounter resource limitations (e.g., scarce labeled data and constrained computing resources). The workshop shall focus on advanced knowledge dicovery techniques used for various applications, with a focus on resource-efficient knowledge discovery and machine learning from three angles: data, model, and system.


Workshop Topics
  • Data-efficient knowledge discovery: Self-supervised/unsupervised learning, semi/weakly-supervised learning, few-shot learning, and their applications to various data modalities (e.g., graph, user behavior, text, web, image, time series) and data science problems (e.g., social media, healthcare, recommendation, finance, multimedia)
  • Algorithm/model-efficient knowledge discovery: Neural network pruning, quantization, acceleration, sparse learning, neural network compression, knowledge distillation, neural architecture search, and their applications on various data science problems.
  • System/hardware-efficient knowledge discovery: Neural network-hardware co-design, real-time and energy-efficient learning system design, hardware accelerators for machine learning, and their applications on various data science problems.
  • Joint-efficient knowledge discovery: Any kind of joint-efficient learning algorithms/methods (e.g., data-model joint learning, algorithm-hardware joint learning) and their application on various data science problems.


Target Audience
The target audiences of this workshop are both academic researchers and industrial practitioners who are new to or interested in resource-efficient knowledge discovery and machine learning as well as those who work on related research. We expect to attract a large number of audiences from different research and engineering backgrounds, including data mining, machine learning, and deep learning.
Workshop Format
To attract a good number of attendees, we would like to design a comprehensive program including (a) invited keynote talks; (b) paper presentations; (c) poster presentations and social session. The workshop is designated as a half-day session.
  • Invited talks: The workshop will have three keynote talks, followed by Q&A. We will invite widely regarded researchers who work on different topics/domains of resource-efficient knowledge discovery.
  • Spotlight oral presentations: There will be workshop paper submissions. After the peer-reviewing process, some selected accepted papers will have spotlight oral presentations.
  • Poster presentations and social session: Each accepted paper will have poster presentation during the social session. We will invite qualified PC members with diverse backgrounds to review the submissions.

Previous Workshops
We have successfully organized similar workshops (i.e., Workshop on Resource-Efficient Learning for Knowledge Discovery) in KDD 2023 and KDD 2024. The workshop attracted more than 50 attendees and about 20 submissions, and received lots of positive feedback from audiences. As a workshop that focuses on resource-efficient knowledge discovery and machine learning, it will bring together researchers and practitioners with related interests in the SDM community. Note that this workshop is different from previously held workshops such as the Data-Efficient Machine Learning Workshop at KDD 2021, since our proposed workshop will cover diverse topics of resource-efficient knowledge discovery in data, model, and system angles. It will also target various techniques and applications of resource-efficient knowledge discovery. To attract more attendees, we will advertise our workshop through the workshop website, mailing lists (e.g., SDM mailing list), and social media (e.g., X/LinkedIn). Since this workshop is highly related to SDM and covers popular and emerging topics in resource-efficient knowledge discovery from data, model, and system levels, we believe it will attract a good number of paper submissions and attendees in SDM 2025.
